Forecasting Bacteria Behavior in the Microbiome

Setting

As a part of my final project for Harvard's CS109b my project group attempted to understand the effects of bacteria in the microbiome, and predict its future makeup. As part of our analysis we analyzed samples drawn from 9 mice over 60 day period, including over 600 different bacteria.

Methodology

Our work started by implementing a Tensorflow ETL pipelines to prepare our data for analysis and model training. We then utilized clustering methods to reduce the dimensionality of our data through using the Hamming Distance of Genetic Sequences and Agglomerative Clustering to determine the clusters, resulting in 140 clusters. We then fitted an Auto Regressive Integrated Moving Average (ARIMA) model and a Vector Autoregressive (VAR) model on the first 50 days of our date and created forectasts on the following 60 days

Argo

Welcome to Argos! Atop a flying reimagining of Odysseys' ship the user is encouraged to charter their own unique journey to Ithaca. As the zeppelin travels, the user will record theirs and Odysseus' unique adventures and trials.

Aiming to promote journaling and mental health, Argos incorporates gaming features to make the process of journaling effortless and easier to stick by. The user can interact and tend to the needs of Odysseus' dog, complete small challenges to maintain the Argos, and grow a garden in the clouds. Progression is tracked by number of diary logs and is encouraged by unlocking new features.

With the ultimate goal of fostering journaling and introspection, Argos attempts to help the user see past their journal entries and reflect on how they have grown as a person as they navigate to their own "Ithaca."
